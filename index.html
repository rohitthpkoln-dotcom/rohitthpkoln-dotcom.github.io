Rohit Mukherjee

I am an Alexander von Humboldt postdoctoral fellow at the Institute of Theoretical Physics, University of Cologne, where I work in Simon Trebst's group on computational condensed matter physics. I completed my PhD at IIT Kanpur under the supervision of Arijit Kundu and Avinash Singh, and during 2023-2025 I was a Fulbright fellow at Cornell in Debanjan Chowdhury's group.

My research lies at the intersection of theoretical and numerical approaches to emergent phenomena in quantum many-body systems. I am particularly interested in thermalization, chaos, measurement-induced phase transitions, quantum error correction, and machine learning. I use tools including exact diagonalization and tensor network techniques, and in earlier work I studied topological transport in quantum magnets and spin liquids, the role of quantum geometry in transport phenomena, and exotic states in Weyl semimetals and topological insulators.

I have also been a visiting student at ICTP in Trieste and at UC Riverside in Ran Cheng's group, and I serve as an active referee and member of the American Physical Society. Across these settings, my work has centered on developing theoretical and numerical tools to understand nonequilibrium quantum dynamics, quantum information, and transport in strongly correlated systems.
